Projects

Here are some of the projects I've on worked in the past, either on my own or as a collaborator. None of these projects are truly 'finished'; I hope to continue adding more features to them as my skills grow.

TicTacToe

A classic game that everyone knows. This was my first project at General Assembly and it was designed to test out knowledge of basic HTML and CSS, as well as Javascript/jQuery. Additional features I added to the game were a score counter and the option for players to choose an emoji for their token.

BarHop

BarHop was built using Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL, Bootstrap, the Google Places API and deployed to Heroku. The app allows users to search a suburb or street and have a list of bars and nightclubs in that area be returned to them in a card format, to make choosing their next social outing easier. Users can also sign up to save bars they like.

Travel Tracker

This was my first group project and we all had a blast building it. Built using React, Rails and 'react-jvectormap'. Developing with React for the first time was tough, so getting the map to behave the way we wanted was a real fist-pump moment. The map allows users to highlight countries they wish to visit or have visited. Note: use email jyeh@ga.co and password 'chicken' to sign in and try the map.

Investment Calculator

When planning this project, I felt my grasp on React wasn't strong enough. I thought the best way to learn something is to just dive in and do it so that's what I did. Using React, Chart.js and the AlphaVantage API, I built an app that can chart the share prices of any american company across different time periods. I also added a second chart with a form that shows you what an investment in that company would look like today.
